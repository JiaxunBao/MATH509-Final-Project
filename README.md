Introduction
Out of the total population of Canada approximately 35.5% of the population holds a mortgage with a total
outstanding amount of $1.8 trillion. And considering the GDP of Canada, which is approximately $2.28–2.39
trillion, outstanding mortgage constitutes roughly 79-82% of the GDP. Any delinquency in the outstanding
mortgage can impact GDP in the form of less household spending, lower investment rates, and many other
effects.
In this paper, we will model & forecast quarterly mortgage delinquency rates across Canadian provinces.
Mortgage delinquency is a key indicator of household financial distress and credit risk. We will use data
of provincial delinquency rates from CMHC along with macro-economic indicators such as unemployment
rate, labor force participation, and interest rate measures. Using Python, we will build a baseline predictive
econometric model and compare them with machine learning models to assess the predictive performance &
interpret the main indicators of delinquency across provinces & time
